What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature, weather patterns, and sea levels. While Earth's climate has changed naturally over time, the current rate of climate change is mainly caused by human activities.

Burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial activities release greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat and cause global warming.

Main Causes of Climate Change

Burning Fossil Fuels

Coal, oil, and gas are used for electricity, transport, and industries. This releases large amounts of CO₂ into the air.

Deforestation

Trees absorb carbon dioxide. Cutting forests reduces this ability and increases greenhouse gases.

Agriculture

Livestock produces methane, a powerful greenhouse gas. Fertilizers also release nitrous oxide.

Industrial Activities

Factories and construction contribute to emissions and pollution, increasing global temperatures.

Real-World Impacts

  • Rising sea levels flooding coastal cities
  • More extreme weather like heatwaves, floods, and hurricanes
  • Melting glaciers and polar ice caps
  • Loss of biodiversity and endangered species
  • Food and water shortages affecting millions of people

UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

SDG 13: Climate Action

Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts by reducing emissions and improving resilience.

SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy

Promotes renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydro power.

SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production

Encourages reducing waste, recycling, and sustainable lifestyles.

SDG 15: Life on Land

Protects forests, wildlife, and ecosystems affected by climate change.

Solutions & What We Can Do

Renewable Energy

Switching to solar, wind, and hydro energy reduces greenhouse gas emissions.

Energy Efficiency

Using LED lights, efficient appliances, and saving electricity helps reduce emissions.

Plant Trees

Afforestation and reforestation absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

Individual Action

Reduce waste, recycle, use public transport, and spread awareness.
